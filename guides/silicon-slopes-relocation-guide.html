Best Places to Live Near Silicon Slopes (Lehi Tech Corridor Guide 2026)

Last Updated: February 2026

Silicon Slopes has transformed Utah County and the southernSalt Lake Valley into one of the fastest-growing tech corridors in the Mountain West. Companies such as Adobe, Qualtrics (legacy presence), and dozens of SaaS startups have anchored long-term job growth in Lehi and surrounding cities.

This guide is designed for professionals, relocating families, and long-distance movers evaluating where to live within commuting distance of the Lehi tech corridor.

Choosing where to live near Silicon Slopes is not simply about proximity. It is a structured decision balancing commute exposure, housing inventory, long-term appreciation potential, lifestyle density, and infrastructure growth.

Who This Guide Is For

  • Tech professionals relocating for roles in Lehi or Draper
  • Remote employees wanting access to the corridor
  • Families balancing schools and commute
  • Investors evaluating growth exposure near The Point development

Understanding The Growth Engine: Silicon Slopes & The Point

The Lehi tech corridor continues expanding north and south along I-15. A major long-term catalyst is The Point development in Draper — the former Utah State Prison site.

The Point is planned as a large-scale mixed-use “15-minute city” concept featuring residential, commercial, office space, retail, and transit connectivity. Over the next 10–20 years, this area is expected to reshape the Draper–Bluffdale corridor.

For relocation strategy, proximity to:

  • I-15
  • Bangerter Highway
  • Mountain View Corridor

will increasingly influence long-term value stability.

Top Cities Near Silicon Slopes

Lehi

Best For: Minimal commute to Silicon Slopes and new construction inventory.

Commute: 5–10 minutes to major tech campuses

Median Home Price: ~$600k–$750k

Typical Home Size: 2,000–3,500 sq ft

Lehi offers the shortest commute and direct corridor access. New construction inventory remains strong, and freeway access is highly convenient.

Pros:

  • Minimal commute exposure
  • Strong tech ecosystem proximity
  • Growing retail and dining options

Considerations:

  • Rapid growth can mean traffic congestion
  • New-build density in certain subdivisions

Draper

Best For: Buyers seeking strong long-term growth exposure near The Point with established infrastructure and canyon proximity.

Commute: 10–15 minutes to Lehi

Median Home Price: ~$800k–$900k

Typical Home Size: 2,500–4,000+ sq ft

Draper combines tech corridor proximity with established neighborhoods and canyon access. It benefits directly from The Point development.

Pros:

  • High elevation views and mountain access
  • Strong long-term development catalysts
  • Well-established community infrastructure

Considerations:

  • Higher entry price point
  • Winter driving conditions in hillside areas

Bluffdale

Best For: Long-term positioning between Draper and Lehi with multi-highway access and emerging development upside.

Commute: 15–20 minutes to Lehi

Median Home Price: ~$650k–$800k

Typical Home Size: 2,500–4,500+ sq ft

Bluffdale sits strategically between Draper and Lehi and may be one of the most undervalued long-term positioning plays in the corridor.

Proximity to I-15, Bangerter Highway, and Mountain View Corridor creates exceptional multi-directional commute flexibility.

Its adjacency to The Point positions it strongly for 10–15 year growth.

Pros:

  • Large homes at lower $/sq ft compared to Draper
  • Strong infrastructure positioning
  • Lower density than central Lehi

Considerations:

  • Retail and dining still developing
  • Less established community core

Riverton

Best For: Families prioritizing space and price efficiency over minimal commute exposure to the tech corridor.

Commute: 20–30 minutes to Lehi

Median Home Price: ~$600k–$700k

Typical Home Size: 2,200–3,800 sq ft

Riverton offers strong suburban living at a slightly lower price point. Some areas benefit from secondary water access, reducing irrigation costs.

Pros:

  • Price-accessible relative to Draper
  • Family-oriented neighborhoods
  • Water cost advantages in certain zones

Considerations:

  • Longer commute during peak hours
  • Less direct exposure to Silicon Slopes growth

In general:

  • Lehi → Lowest commute friction
  • Draper → Balanced
  • Bluffdale → Strategic flexibility
  • Riverton → Slightly longer exposure

Strategic Takeaways

  • Lehi: Best for immediate commute minimization.
  • Draper: Balanced growth + established infrastructure.
  • Bluffdale: Strong 10–15 year positioning adjacent to The Point.
  • Riverton: Budget-aligned suburban option.

Your optimal choice depends on whether commute minimization, price per square foot, long-term development exposure, or lifestyle density carries the most weight.

Conclusion

Silicon Slopes continues expanding south and north along the corridor. Areas positioned near multi-highway access and The Point development may see strong long-term structural demand.

Relocation decisions near the tech corridor should prioritize infrastructure exposure, budget stability, and commute resilience rather than trend-driven popularity.
